# Astoria Hospitality — Website Audit Report
**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**URL:** https://astoriahosp.com  
**Client:** Elissar Maalouf — CEO & Partner  
**Markets:** Oman (Muscat) + Lebanon (Beirut)

---

## Executive Summary

The current website is a purchased template with minimal customization. It actively undermines Astoria's credibility despite the company having a strong client portfolio (Four Seasons, Kempinski, InterContinental, Radisson…) and 25+ premium brand partnerships. The site is invisible on search engines and contains placeholder/fake data that makes the business look unprofessional.

---

## Critical Problems (Must Fix)

### P1 — Fake/Placeholder Data
- **Footer address:** "Jl. Raya Kuta No.70, Kuta" — this is in **Bali, Indonesia**. Not Oman. Not Lebanon.
- **Footer email:** `support@domain.com` — a default template email, not a real contact
- **Footer phone:** `(+021) 1580 3658` — template placeholder number
- **Impact:** Any visitor who scrolls down sees immediately that this is a template. Destroys trust.

### P2 — Fake Team Members
- The About page lists: "Melisa Goff - Marketing Expert", "Adem Chaer - CEO Socialzy", "Brodie Perez - Marketing Expert"
- These are **template dummy names** with stock photos. None are real Astoria employees.
- **Impact:** If a potential client (hotel procurement manager) checks the team page, they see fake people. Catastrophic for a B2B relationship business.

### P3 — Broken Pages
- `/services` returns a **404 error** — the page doesn't exist
- This is linked from the main navigation, so visitors hit a dead end
- **Impact:** Broken navigation = unprofessional + lost visitor

### P4 — No Value Proposition
- No tagline, no positioning statement, no "why Astoria"
- Hero section only shows the logo + "HOTEL & RESTAURANT SUPPLIES" — generic descriptor
- **Impact:** Visitor can't differentiate Astoria from any other supplier in 5 seconds

---

## Major Problems (High Priority)

### P5 — Zero SEO Foundation
- No meta titles/descriptions optimized for keywords
- No H1/H2 heading hierarchy with target keywords
- No structured data (Schema.org — LocalBusiness, Organization)
- No blog or content pages
- No sitemap.xml visible
- **Impact:** Astoria is invisible on Google. Searching "hospitality supplier Oman" or "hotel equipment supplier Muscat" won't return Astoria. In a market where NO competitor does SEO well, this is a massive missed opportunity.

### P6 — No Social Proof / Case Studies
- 21 hotel clients are shown as a logo grid with zero context
- No project photos, no descriptions, no "we supplied X for Y hotel"
- No testimonials or quotes from hotel clients
- **Impact:** The strongest selling point (world-class hotel clients) is reduced to tiny logos. No storytelling, no proof of work quality.

### P7 — Generic Vision/Mission Content
- Vision: "We do not seek to prepare projects only, but we look at the customer as a success partner" — corporate filler
- Mission: "provide the best services to our customers" — could be any company in any industry
- **Impact:** Wasted page that adds no value or differentiation

### P8 — Brand Partners Underutilized
- 25 premium brands (Churchill, Fortessa, Schott, Cutipol, Costa Nova…) shown as logo grid only
- No descriptions of what each brand offers, why it was chosen, or which hospitality segment it serves
- **Impact:** A procurement manager looking for Churchill tableware in Oman won't find Astoria through search, and won't understand the partnership depth from the current page.

---

## Design & UX Problems

### P9 — Template Design Not Customized
- Website was built by "Image Matters EN / Creation Image FR" using a template
- Default template structure, default animations, default section layouts
- Copyright still says 2024
- **Impact:** Looks generic. In an industry where aesthetics = credibility (hotels judge suppliers by their own presentation), a template site signals "we don't invest in our image."

### P10 — No Clear CTAs
- No "Request a Quote" button
- No "Download Catalog" option
- No "Schedule a Consultation" prompt
- Contact page exists but nothing drives visitors there
- **Impact:** Even if someone lands on the site, there's no conversion path

### P11 — Inconsistent Color Application
- Navy + gold palette is actually good (luxury hospitality standard)
- But purple header overlay competes with navy
- Gold accent is inconsistent — different shades across elements
- **Impact:** Minor, but the palette could be more polished

### P12 — No Mobile Optimization Evidence
- Template may be technically responsive, but no evidence of mobile-first design
- Product images and logo grids likely break on mobile
- **Impact:** Hotel procurement managers increasingly browse on phones/tablets

## Content Problems

### P13 — Two Sets of Contact Info
- Real contacts (from /contact page): Oman +968 99326424, Lebanon +961 3078727
- Fake contacts (from footer on every page): Bali address, template email/phone
- **Impact:** Contradictory info. Which one is real? Visitor confusion.

### P14 — No Product Detail
- Products dropdown exists but pages are thin — category names and images, no specs
- No filtering, no brand association, no use-case guidance
- **Impact:** Can't serve as a product reference. Visitors must call to learn anything.

### P15 — Social Media Disconnect
- Facebook handle is `horecafield` — doesn't match the brand name "Astoria Hospitality"
- Instagram is `astoria_hospitality` — better but not linked prominently
- No feed integration, no content cross-posting strategy
- **Impact:** Fragmented brand presence online
